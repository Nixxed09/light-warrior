# Light Warrior Systems And Progression

This is the deeper Lila structure for Light Warrior: how progression, enemies, weapons, resources, lessons, and cause/effect fit the main game.

Core idea stays unchanged:

```text
Bravery expands the light.
```

The game is about reclaiming Old Earth nodes through action. Lila makes every action teach a law through immediate cause and effect.

## Progression Shape

Light Warrior should progress through restored nodes, not abstract levels.

| Stage | Player state | World state | Main lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. First Spark | The player learns movement, dash, strike, circle safety. | One dead Thunder Hammer node flickers. | Bravery changes the field. |
| 2. Field Tuning | The player learns to leave safety on purpose. | Circle expands into visible resonance lines. | Like calls to like. |
| 3. Temple Chain | The player restores multiple weapon temples. | Nearby route lines and true-map fragments wake. | The world is a circuit. |
| 4. Cause Breaking | The player targets enemy conditions, not only bodies. | Suppression fields collapse; corrupted zones become usable. | Remove the cause and the shape dissolves. |
| 5. True Map | The player sees hidden routes and node relations. | The flat world-plane opens outward through routes, not edges. | Sight rises with octave. |
| 6. Radiant Node | The player holds a large field under pressure. | The node becomes a living sanctuary and launch point. | Remembering restores the old system. |

First playable only needs Stage 1. Vertical slice should clearly hint at Stage 2 and Stage 3.

## Resources

Resources should be physical expressions of the laws, not generic currencies.

| Resource | Earned by | Spent on | Law | Design note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Light | Defeating shadows, restoring circle edge, temple activation. | Circle expansion, healing pulses, temporary safe paths. | Resonance, Mirrors | Primary combat resource; mostly automatic, visible in-world. |
| Courage | Leaving the circle, surviving darkness, reaching temples under pressure. | Weapon temple activation, high-risk upgrades, octave rises. | Consent, Remembering | Tracks brave action, not grind. |
| Resonance Shards | Converting enemies, hitting groups with temple weapons. | Tune weapon temples and node machinery. | Resonance, Poles | Drop as luminous fragments, not coins. |
| True-Map Fragments | Restoring node geometry, winning runs, finding route lines. | Unlock new arenas/routes. | Remembering, Mirrors | Progression resource for world expansion. |
| Shadow Debt | Waiting passively, abandoning breaches, taking corrupt bargains. | Must be cleared through restoration or brave play. | Return, Consent | A pressure resource; it creates consequences, not punishment. |
| Aether Charge | Standing in restored geometry, timing attacks with field pulses. | Heavy weapon attacks, route gates, sanctuary machines. | Tides, Resonance | Advanced resource after first playable. |

### Resource Rule

Every resource must answer:

```text
What did the player do to cause this?
What visible world change proves the effect?
```

If the answer is unclear, the resource is not ready.

## Weapon Temples

Weapons should be sacred technologies tied to different laws. They are not loot drops. They are node functions the player reawakens.

| Weapon temple | Combat role | Law | Lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thunder Hammer | Shockwave, crowd clear, dramatic circle push. | Resonance | The field answers a brave strike. |
| Sun Spear | Line pierce, route-line activation, precision. | Mind | Focus chooses what manifests. |
| Bell Shield | Parry, reflected pulses, safe breach holding. | Mirrors | What arrives can be reflected cleanly. |
| Vortex Blade | Dash arcs, enemy repositioning, flow attacks. | Tides, Yielding | Move with the current, not against it. |
| Mercy Flame | Converts weakened shadows into light allies or field charge. | Poles, Arising | Darkness can be raised when its cause is broken. |
| Star Bow | Long-range shots along restored lines. | Remembering | True sight makes distant routes reachable. |

### Weapon Rule

Every weapon must tempt the player outside safety and then change positioning decisions. If it only raises damage, it is too shallow.

## Enemy Families

Enemies should embody control pressures that oppose the laws. Each family teaches a counter-law through play.

| Enemy | Behavior | What it teaches | Counter-law |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shadow Imp | Rushes, harasses, dies in light burst. | Strike darkness and the field grows. | Resonance |
| Berserker Demon | Telegraphs charge, punishes panic dodges. | Fear narrows movement; clarity creates openings. | Mind |
| Suppressor | Projects a field that shrinks the circle faster. | Find the cause, not the symptom. | Arising |
| Tether Wraith | Links to player and drains light while ignored. | What you consent to keeps feeding. | Consent |
| Mirror Shade | Copies reckless attacks and returns them. | Your pattern comes back to you. | Return, Mirrors |
| Null Priest | Silences temple geometry until interrupted. | The old grid must be defended. | Remembering |
| Debt Collector | Spawns stronger if Shadow Debt grows. | Passive choices create future pressure. | Return |
| Gate Eater | Consumes true-map route lines. | Sight must be protected. | Remembering |

### Enemy Rule

An enemy is strongest when the player plays unconsciously and weakest when the player understands the law it embodies.

## Cause And Effect Loops

These loops should be readable in one run.

| Cause | Immediate effect | Delayed effect | Lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leave safety to reach temple | Darkness damages player, temple activates. | Circle expands farther than a safe kill would. | Brave risk returns power. |
| Wait inside circle too long | Circle shrinks, enemies gather. | Shadow Debt rises; future wave gets harder. | Passive safety is not freedom. |
| Kill enemy inside restored field | Light burst expands circle. | Resonance Shards collect for temple tuning. | Like strengthens like. |
| Break a Suppressor field | Shrink pressure stops. | Hidden route line appears. | Remove the cause. |
| Use Thunder Hammer on a cluster | Huge burst, strong feedback. | Node geometry wakes. | The old world is a circuit. |
| Repeated reckless attacks | Mirror Shade copies pattern. | Returned damage teaches timing. | What you send out comes back. |
| Convert a weakened enemy | Field charge instead of drop. | Mercy Flame path unlocks. | Raise the pole. |

## Lesson Structure

The game should teach through play first, naming second.

| Lesson | First felt as | Later named as |
|---|---|---|
| Bravery expands the light. | Circle grows after risky success. | Law of Resonance / Mirrors |
| Waiting safely can still lose ground. | Circle shrinks under pressure. | Law of Consent |
| The enemy has a cause. | Suppressor must be broken before clearing is easy. | Law of Arising |
| Your pattern returns. | Mirror Shade copies reckless behavior. | Law of Return |
| Darkness can be raised. | Mercy Flame converts instead of destroys. | Law of Poles |
| Sight is earned. | Route lines and tells become visible after restoration. | Law of Remembering / Octave |

The first playable should name nothing during combat. A post-run result can reveal one short title after the player has already felt it.

## Node Progression

Each node should have:

- one sanctuary field
- one main weapon temple
- one visible route lock
- one enemy family that embodies the node's distortion
- one restored-state visual transformation
- one law that becomes named after mastery

Example node chain:

| Node | Weapon | Main enemy | Resource focus | Law named |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thunder Hammer Node | Thunder Hammer | Shadow Imp / Berserker | Light, Courage | Resonance |
| Bell Gate Node | Bell Shield | Mirror Shade | Shadow Debt | Mirrors |
| Broken Cause Node | Mercy Flame | Suppressor / Tether Wraith | Resonance Shards | Arising |
| Vortex Canal Node | Vortex Blade | Gate Eater | Aether Charge | Tides |
| Star Route Node | Star Bow | Null Priest | True-Map Fragments | Remembering |

## Run Progression

Short-run progression:

1. Start in small circle.
2. Kill one weak enemy and see circle grow.
3. Darkness pressure starts shrinking the field.
4. Temple appears as the obvious risk.
5. Player reaches temple and gets weapon spike.
6. Weapon clears wave and expands circle.
7. Result screen names the strongest law the player caused.

Long-run progression:

1. Restore node.
2. Earn true-map fragment.
3. Choose neighboring node.
4. Carry one tuned temple blessing.
5. Face a new enemy family built around a new law.
6. Return to old nodes at higher octave and see hidden routes.

## Upgrade Categories

| Upgrade type | Examples | Law |
|---|---|---|
| Circle tuning | larger start radius, slower shrink, breach cleanse pulse | Mirrors, Consent |
| Courage abilities | dash trail leaves temporary safe path, first temple reach grants shield | Remembering |
| Weapon tuning | hammer echoes, spear line opens routes, bell reflects projectiles | Resonance, Mirrors |
| Octave sight | clearer tells, visible tethers, true-map overlays | Remembering |
| Conversion path | weakened enemies become light, corrupt pools become charge | Poles, Arising |

Upgrade rule: no upgrade should erase risk. Upgrades should make brave action more expressive.

## AI Use In Progression

The Luminous Witness can help progression in three restrained ways:

- post-run: name the law the player just demonstrated
- map: mark one optional visible route or temple
- recap: reflect the player's strongest pattern that run

It should not give build advice, combat orders, or hidden solutions in the first playable.

## First Build Target

For the current UE5 migration, build only:

- Light
- Courage
- Thunder Hammer
- Shadow Imp
- Berserker Demon
- circle expansion/shrink
- authored Luminous Witness fallback
- one post-run law naming hook

Everything else is expansion after Play-In-Editor evidence.
