# Reference Games

## Hook Lens

Light Warrior is not trying to be "like Hades" or "like Vampire Survivors" in general. Each reference must be studied through the hook:

```text
Bravery expands the light.
```

Reference notes should only become production guidance when they help the player read safety, temptation, risk, power, and reclaimed territory faster.

## Primary Proven Reference

- Reference: Hades
- What players already understand: readable arena combat, dash/attack rhythm, enemy tells, fast retries.
- Mechanics to study: combat spacing, arena clarity, enemy telegraphs, upgrade pacing.
- First 10 seconds to study: immediate movement, attack, enemy response, reward feedback.
- Feel/readability lessons: strong silhouettes and fast feedback keep combat understandable.
- What not to copy: isometric-only structure; Light Warrior targets UE5 third-person combat.

## Better Claim

The arena itself responds to courage: light expands when the player fights well, shrinks when darkness wins, and makes bravery visible as territory.

## New Hook

Weapon temples sit outside safety, making bravery the core interaction. The player should never read temples as ordinary pickups; they are promises that danger can become power.

## Reference Capture Checklist

When capturing reference evidence, record:

- How quickly the reference shows a safe/actionable space.
- How it tempts the player into danger.
- How reward feedback makes the risk feel worth it.
- How camera, VFX, and audio protect combat readability.
- What Light Warrior should do differently so bravery changing territory is unmistakable.

## Decision

- Build now: movement, dash, light strike, sacred circle, one temple.
- Refine before build: camera distance and circle boundary readability.
- Park until signal: bosses, full weapon roster, meta progression.
