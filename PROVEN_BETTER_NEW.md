# Proven Better New

## Decision

Light Warrior remains a valid GamesOS concept. The rethink is about production discipline, not replacing the idea.

The current browser build is a `throwaway_prototype`. The production game must move to UE5 before any task is counted as first-playable or vertical-slice production work.

## Proven Pattern

Target player expectation:

- Readable arena combat.
- Dash, strike, reposition rhythm.
- Enemies with visible tells.
- Short survival pressure curve.
- Fast recovery after mistakes.

Primary references:

- `Hades`: arena readability, dash/attack cadence, enemy spacing, fast combat feedback.
- `Vampire Survivors`: mounting pressure and the readable survival loop.
- `Risk of Rain 2`: escalation, survival mastery, and replayable pressure.

Quality references:

- `Kena: Bridge of Spirits`: heroic magical tone, readable light/dark contrast, cinematic but approachable fantasy.
- `Hades`: encounter clarity and feedback economy.

Do not copy:

- Hades' exact isometric structure.
- Vampire Survivors' passive auto-combat.
- Generic fantasy arena presentation.

## Explicit Hook

```text
Bravery expands the light.
```

The player is safest inside the sacred circle, but the power needed to win is outside it. The game is about deciding to step into danger, proving courage through combat, and seeing the world become safer because of that choice.

## Better Claim

The arena is not just a backdrop. The sacred light circle is safety, progress, score, and pressure feedback in one readable system.

Player-visible improvement:

- A target player can see the world getting safer because they fought bravely.
- Successful action expands the circle.
- Losing control shrinks the circle.
- The boundary makes risk legible without a tutorial wall.

First five minutes proof:

- The player starts inside light.
- The player kills a shadow enemy.
- The circle expands immediately.
- The player steps outside light and takes understandable damage.
- The player chooses to leave safety for Thunder Hammer.

## New Hook

Weapon temples sit outside the safe circle, so the player must leave safety to become strong enough to reclaim more ground. The hook is not complete unless the player understands that brave action directly expands the light.

One-sentence hook:

> A heroic arena roguelite where bravery expands the light: leave the sacred circle, claim a weapon temple, and return strong enough to reclaim the arena.

Trailer moment:

- Player dashes out of the shrinking light circle.
- Shadow enemies close in.
- Thunder Hammer activates at the temple.
- The player slams the ground.
- A light shockwave destroys enemies and expands the circle.

## Fallback Hooks

If the temple-risk hook is weak:

- Fallback A: Light trails reward brave routes and become temporary safe paths.
- Fallback B: Enemies corrupt the circle edge, forcing the player to cleanse specific breach points.

Stable loop if hooks change:

- Third-person movement.
- Dash/strike combat.
- Light circle territory system.
- Readable enemy pressure.

## Build Purpose

Current build:

- Type: `throwaway_prototype`
- Question: Does the sacred-circle risk/reward loop read quickly enough to justify UE5 production?
- Not polishing: browser architecture, final art, final audio, progression, more weapons.
- Hook proof: a first-time player can say that leaving safety made the light stronger.

Next production build:

- Type: `first_playable`
- Engine: UE5
- Question: Does the first 10 seconds feel like the foundation of a real third-person arena action game?
- Hook proof: the captured clip shows safety, temptation, risk, power, and circle expansion without explanation.
