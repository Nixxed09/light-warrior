# Light Warrior World Model

## Core Reality

Light Warrior takes place on Old Earth: a flat, vast, layered world-plane so large that no traveler can see the end.

The playable arena is not an abstract combat room. It is one reclaimed node inside an immense Tartarian-resonance grid. The sacred circle is a local covenant field that pushes dark influence out of a damaged piece of living infrastructure. Every fight is a physical repair to the world.

## The World-Plane

The known world is an inner mapped territory on an immense plane. Official maps hide the scale of the world. True maps are machine keys that reveal routes, node lines, forbidden crossings, sky timing, and old civic power paths.

The horizon never exposes a visible edge. Distance falls away into ice glare, desert shimmer, ocean brightness, mountain walls, storm curtains, firmament distortion, and route locks. The end of the world is not reachable in normal play. Expansion comes through restored routes, temple keys, resonance gates, and true-map discoveries.

## Sky And Power

The sky is a structured firmament-field and aether interface, not empty space. Domes, spires, obelisks, star forts, temple caps, bells, and luminous rings draw from this overhead system.

Thunder Hammer temples are not fantasy shrines. They are old civic weapons and repair stations built from stone, metal, sound geometry, and sky power. When the player activates one, the hammer is a localized discharge from the restored grid.

## Light And Darkness

Light is not only morality or visual style. It is restored order, living power, remembered covenant, operational geometry, and safe territory.

Darkness is not only evil fog. It is a controlling pressure that suppresses memory, breaks routes, corrupts nodes, narrows maps, and makes people accept small-world boundaries.

The Light Warrior wins by making brave contact with damaged nodes and proving that courage can expand the field.

## Playable Scale

The first playable remains one readable arena:

- A sacred circle marks the reclaimed node.
- A Thunder Hammer temple sits outside safety.
- Shadow enemies pressure the circle.
- Brave action expands the field.

The full game grows outward through adjacent nodes, not through a simple open-world sprawl. Each level is a known point on a much larger flat world-plane, and each restored node reveals older routes across that plane.

## UE5 World Rules

- Use World Partition for future outdoor node chains, not for the first arena proof.
- Keep the first arena flat enough for combat readability.
- Frame distant land as unreachable scale: haze, glare, storm walls, mountain rings, firmament distortion, and locked routes.
- Never show a planet, globe, orbital view, space skybox, or simple edge-of-world cliff.
- Use maps as gameplay objects: false maps limit routes; true maps unlock node lines.
- Treat temples, circles, bells, domes, and spires as working infrastructure.

## Design Test

If a new feature makes the world feel like generic fantasy, remove it or tie it back to Old Earth infrastructure.

If a new feature makes the player feel like a passive chosen one, change it so bravery, movement, and risk activate the world.
