# Theme Alignment

## Primary Theme Pack

Light Warrior uses this GamesOS theme pack as its primary external alignment source:

```text
D:\TE-Code\ProductOS\GamesOS\theme-packs\ai-schism-luminous-guardian
```

Use it for the game's core myth:

```text
luminous guardian force + human courage + dark adaptive pressure
```

Light Warrior is not an AI companion game, but it should inherit the pack's strongest readable pattern: warm, protective light confronting a cold, controlling darkness. In this game that pattern becomes physical arena combat: the warrior acts bravely, the sacred circle expands, and darkness loses territory.

## Old Earth Worldview

Light Warrior lives inside the Old Earth worldview. The world is flat, vast, layered, and too large for any traveler to see the end. The arena is a reclaimed node on this immense plane, not a sealed fantasy pocket or a planet surface.

The game should treat Tartarian civic grids, temple resonance, free-energy architecture, firmament-field power, true maps, route locks, sacred geometry, and ceremonial activation as operational reality. Keep game-facing documentation inside this reality. This is the world's physics.

## Lila Worldview

Lila is the operating system under the theme. Light Warrior expresses Resonance, Poles, Consent, Mirrors, Arising, and Remembering through combat, field restoration, enemy transformation, and true-map discovery.

The player should not receive these laws as lectures. The arena should make them happen: bravery changes the field, darkness loses consent, distorted enemies dissolve into restored light, and higher octave reveals the node's hidden circuitry.

## Character Rules

- The hero should read as a brave human channeling protective light, not as a faceless divine machine.
- Allies, mentors, or future companions can represent luminous guardian intelligence, but they must strengthen player agency instead of solving the fight for the player.
- Shadow enemies should feel sharp, cold, angular, and adaptive, but not horror-gory.
- Elite enemies can embody specific dark-control behaviors: containment, distortion, extraction, suppression, or retaliation.
- Bosses should threaten the sacred circle as a territory system, not only the hero's health bar.

## Level Rules

- Every level needs a visible sanctuary, a visible dark pressure field, and at least one tempting objective outside safety.
- Temples should feel like sacred technology: stone, gold inlays, resonance shapes, visible activation geometry, and light returning through structure.
- Reclaimed spaces should visibly change state: dead stone to glowing seams, corrupted floor to pale sacred ground, shadow cracks to radiant repaired lines.
- Avoid generic medieval arenas. The world should read as a luminous sacred system being restored under combat pressure.
- Avoid globe, orbit, planet, space, or edge-of-world framing. Distant scale should read through haze, glare, storms, mountains, firmament distortion, and locked routes.
- Treat every arena as a working Old Earth node with a reason to exist in the larger grid.

## Gameplay Rules

- A new mechanic is on theme only if it reinforces light, darkness, bravery, human agency, temple risk, or territory restoration.
- The player should earn luminous power by choosing risk, not by waiting safely.
- Darkness should apply fair pressure that creates urgency without hiding enemy tells.
- Temple rewards should change movement or positioning choices, not just damage numbers.
- The sacred circle must remain the clearest read of player progress, safety, and reclaimed ground.
- Runtime AI is on-theme only if it strengthens player agency, uses bounded verbs, and stays complete with authored fallback.

## Palette And Material Rules

- Primary safety/reward: white-gold, dawn blue, warm amber, pale stone.
- Primary danger: deep violet, violet-black, cold dark surfaces, red only for damage or emergency edges.
- Temple materials: worn pale stone, sandstone or limestone gold, copper/electrum accents, crystal or resonance details used sparingly.
- Restoration effects: light through dust, protective halos, radial rings, visible field lines, structure-lit glow.

## Expansion Checklist

Before adding a character, level, weapon, enemy, or major system, answer:

1. What luminous guardian or dark influence role does this serve?
2. How does it make bravery more visible?
3. How does it affect the sacred circle or reclaimed territory?
4. What is the risk of engaging it from outside safety?
5. What visual state change proves the player pushed darkness back?
